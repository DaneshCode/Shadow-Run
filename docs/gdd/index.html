1 Game Overview

1.1 Game Title

SHADOW RUN: Endless Nightmare

1.2 Genre

Horror-themed Endless Runner with Action and Shooter elements. The game combines the fast-paced, reflex-driven gameplay of classic endless runners with dark horror aesthetics, strategic shooting mechanics, and a unique gravity-flip system.

1.3 Platform(s)

  • Primary: Desktop (Windows, macOS, Linux)
  • Display: 1280×720 native resolution, scalable
  • Input: Keyboard and Mouse

1.4 Target Audience

Demographics

  • Age Range: 13+ (Teen to Adult)
  • Player Type: Casual to mid-core gamers who enjoy arcade-style games with high replayability
  • Interests: Horror themes, fast-paced action, high-score chasing, reflex-based gameplay

The game appeals to players who enjoy titles such as Canabalt, Jetpack Joyride, Limbo, and Dead Cells—combining accessibility with depth and a distinct visual atmosphere.

1.5 Core Concept & Unique Hook

Elevator Pitch

"Run through an endless nightmare, flipping between ground and ceiling to survive waves of horrific creatures in a dark, atmospheric world where every second counts."

Key Differentiators

  • Gravity Flip Mechanic: Players can instantly flip between the ground and ceiling, opening unique traversal and combat strategies
  • Strategic Shooting: Limited ammunition with regeneration forces players to choose engagements carefully
  • Invisibility System: A charge-based stealth mechanic allows tactical evasion
  • Progressive Horror: Difficulty scales smoothly with score, introducing new enemy types and patterns
  • Dark Atmospheric Design: A cohesive horror visual identity with parallax backgrounds, fog effects, and ambient audio

1.6 Design Pillars

🎮 Accessibility

Simple controls that are easy to learn but offer depth through mastery of timing and positioning.

⚡ Tension

Constant forward momentum and limited resources create persistent pressure and excitement.

🔄 Replayability

Procedural generation, leaderboards, and progressive difficulty ensure every run feels fresh.

🌑 Atmosphere

Dark horror aesthetics and ambient audio create an immersive nightmare experience.

2 Core Gameplay

2.1 Endless Runner Structure

Shadow Run follows the endless runner paradigm where the player character automatically moves forward through a procedurally generated world. The game continues indefinitely until the player's health reaches zero.

Core Loop

  1. Run: The player automatically moves forward at an increasing pace
  2. React: Obstacles and enemies approach; player must respond
  3. Survive: Use jumping, shooting, and gravity-flipping to stay alive
  4. Score: Distance traveled and enemies defeated contribute to score
  5. Repeat: Death leads to score submission and restart option

2.2 Automatic Forward Movement

Parameter Value Description
Base Auto-Run Speed 4 units/frame Initial horizontal movement speed
Base Game Speed 1.0× Starting multiplier for all game systems
Maximum Game Speed 2.0× Cap on speed scaling to maintain playability
Speed Curve Steepness 0.0003 Controls how gradually speed increases

Game speed increases smoothly based on the player's score, creating natural difficulty progression without jarring transitions.

2.3 Jumping Mechanics

Jump System

  • Jump Power: -16 units (upward velocity)
  • Gravity: 0.8 units/frame²
  • Maximum Fall Speed: 18 units/frame
  • Ground Detection: Collision-based with platform surfaces

Jumping is the primary defensive action, allowing players to avoid ground-based enemies and hazards. The jump arc is designed to feel responsive yet weighty, giving players precise control over their trajectory.

2.4 Shooting Mechanics

Projectile System

Bullet Speed 18 units/frame
Bullet Damage 50 HP
Bullet Size 20 pixels
Cooldown 250ms between shots
Bullet Lifetime 2000ms
Maximum Ammo 5 bullets
Ammo Regeneration 1 bullet per 10 seconds

The limited ammunition system forces strategic decision-making. Players must evaluate threats and choose when to engage versus when to evade, adding a layer of resource management to the reflex-based gameplay.

2.5 Gravity Flip Mechanic

Signature Feature

The gravity flip mechanic is the defining feature that sets Shadow Run apart from traditional endless runners.

How It Works

  1. Player activates gravity flip via input
  2. Gravity direction inverts (ground ↔ ceiling)
  3. Player character rotates 180° during transition
  4. Player lands on the opposite surface
Flip Transition Speed 25 units
Flip Cooldown 100ms minimum between flips
Transition Duration 15 frames animation
Ceiling Height 100 pixels (mirrors ground)

Strategic Applications

  • Evade ground enemies by flipping to ceiling
  • Access ceiling-only paths and collectibles
  • Engage ceiling enemies on their terrain
  • Create vertical distance from projectiles
  • Chain flips for advanced traversal patterns

2.6 Risk vs. Reward Philosophy

Design Intent

Every player action involves weighing potential gains against dangers:

  • Shooting: Eliminates threats but depletes limited ammo
  • Gravity Flip: Evades current threats but may introduce new ones
  • Collecting Coins: Increases score but may require risky positioning
  • Using Invisibility: Provides safety but has long charge time

Design Goals

  • Minimal button count (3 core actions)
  • Immediate response to input

  • Left-hand keyboard, right-hand mouse layout

Accessibility Considerations

  • Both hands can perform all actions
  • No simultaneous button presses required
  • Clear visual feedback for all inputs
  • Customizable controls (future)

4 Enemies

4.1 Enemy Overview

Enemies in Shadow Run are designed to create varied challenges that test different player skills. Each enemy type has distinct visual design, behavior patterns, and threat levels.

Base Stats Value
Enemy Size 100×100 pixels
Base Speed 2 units/frame
Base Damage 20 HP
Spawn Distance 800 pixels ahead of player

4.2 Ground-Based Enemies

🔴 Basic Enemy (Slime)

Simple patrol enemy that moves back and forth within a set range.

Health 1 HP (one-shot)
Damage 20 HP
Behavior Horizontal patrol
Patrol Range 100 pixels
Unlock Score 0 (available from start)

🟠 Chaser Enemy

Aggressive enemy that detects the player and pursues at high speed.

Health 1 HP
Damage 30 HP (1.5× base)
Detection Range 300 pixels
Chase Speed 4 units/frame (2× base)
Unlock Score 500 points

🔵 Shooter Enemy (Turret)

Stationary enemy that fires projectiles at the player.

Health 1 HP
Projectile Damage 10 HP
Detection Range 400 pixels
Fire Rate 1 shot per 2 seconds
Projectile Speed 6 units/frame
Unlock Score 1500 points

🟣 Berserker Enemy

Large, dangerous enemy that charges at the player after a brief wind-up.

Health 3 HP (requires multiple hits)
Damage 40 HP (2× base)
Detection Range 500 pixels
Charge Speed 8 units/frame (4× base)
Wind-up Time 30 frames (~0.5 seconds)
Charge Duration 90 frames (~1.5 seconds)
Unlock Score 3000 points

👻 Ghost Enemy

Ethereal enemy that phases in and out of visibility. Immune to damage when phased.

Health 1 HP (when visible)
Damage 24 HP (1.2× base)
Speed 1.6 units/frame (0.8× base)
Phase Cycle Continuous sine wave
Immunity Threshold Alpha < 100

4.3 Ceiling-Based Enemies

Ceiling enemies mirror ground enemy types but are positioned on the ceiling, creating threats when the player uses gravity flip.

  • Ceiling Basic: Patrol enemy attached to ceiling
  • Ceiling Chaser: Pursues player along ceiling surface
  • Ceiling Shooter: Inverted turret firing downward
  • Ceiling Ghost: Phasing enemy on ceiling

Design Note

Ceiling enemies use a purple-tinted color palette (80, 40, 120) to visually distinguish them from ground enemies.

4.4 Flying Enemies

🟢 Flying Enemy

Enemy that moves through the air, threatening both ground and ceiling positions.

Color Green-tinted (60, 100, 80)
Speed 3 units/frame (1.5× base)
Movement Sine-wave vertical oscillation

🕷️ Spider Enemy

Drops from ceiling or rises from ground when player approaches.

🌀 Teleporter Enemy

Blinks between positions, creating unpredictable threats.

4.5 Enemy Behavior Patterns

Individual Behaviors

  • Patrol: Back-and-forth movement
  • Chase: Pursue player when detected
  • Shoot: Fire projectiles at player
  • Charge: Wind-up then rush attack
  • Phase: Cycle visibility/immunity

Group Patterns

  • Swarm: 3-6 enemies spawned together
  • Swarm Spacing: 120 pixels apart
  • Swarm Unlock: 5000+ score
  • Swarm Chance: 0% → 25% (scales)

4.6 Difficulty Progression

Enemy spawning and variety scales smoothly with player score:

Score Range Enemy Types Weights (Basic/Chaser/Shooter/Berserker)
0 – 500 Basic only 100% / 0% / 0% / 0%
500 – 1500 Basic, Chaser 60% / 40% / 0% / 0%
1500 – 3000 Basic, Chaser, Shooter 40% / 35% / 25% / 0%
3000 – 5000 All types 25% / 30% / 25% / 20%
5000+ All types + Swarms 15% / 25% / 30% / 30%

Spawn Rate Scaling

Base Spawn Interval 500 pixels
Minimum Spawn Interval 150 pixels (at max difficulty)
Base Spawn Chance 40%
Maximum Spawn Chance 85%
Enemy Speed Multiplier 1.0× → 2.5×

5 Game Systems

5.1 Health System

Maximum Health 100 HP
Starting Health 100 HP
Invincibility on Hit 1500ms (1.5 seconds)
Health Pack Restore 25 HP

Damage Sources

  • Contact with basic enemies: 20 HP
  • Contact with chasers: 30 HP
  • Contact with berserkers: 40 HP
  • Enemy projectiles: 10 HP

Invincibility Frames

After taking damage, the player becomes temporarily invincible and blinks to indicate this state. This prevents chain-damage scenarios and gives players recovery time.

5.2 Ammo System

Ammunition Management

Maximum Ammunition 5 bullets
Starting Ammunition 5 bullets
Regeneration Rate 1 bullet per 10 seconds
Regeneration Type Passive (always regenerating)

The limited ammo pool with slow regeneration creates meaningful resource decisions. Players cannot rely solely on shooting and must incorporate jumping and gravity-flipping into their survival strategy.

5.3 Invisibility System

Stealth Mechanic

Invisibility Duration 3 seconds when activated
Charge Time 15 seconds to fully charge
Player Alpha (Invisible) 80 / 255 (semi-transparent)

While invisible, enemies cannot detect or damage the player. This provides an emergency escape option but requires careful timing due to the long charge period.

5.4 Score System

Score Sources

  • Distance: Points accumulated based on distance traveled
  • Coins: 10 points per coin collected
  • Enemies: Points for eliminating enemies
  • Survival Time: Bonus points for staying alive

Difficulty Milestones

Every 1000 points triggers a difficulty milestone notification, providing feedback on progression without disrupting gameplay.

5.5 High Score Persistence

Leaderboard System

  • Storage: JSON file (data/leaderboard.json)
  • Maximum Entries: 10 scores
  • Data Stored: Player name, score, date
  • Display: Accessible from main menu

5.6 Difficulty Scaling System

The difficulty system uses smooth mathematical curves rather than discrete levels, creating imperceptible increases that feel natural.

Parameter Scaling Method Range
Game Speed Ease-out quadratic 1.0× → 2.0×
Spawn Interval Ease-in quadratic 500 → 150 pixels
Spawn Chance Linear interpolation 40% → 85%
Enemy Speed Linear interpolation 1.0× → 2.5×

Maximum Difficulty Score: 10,000 points (soft cap; continues slowly beyond)

Maximum Difficulty Multiplier: 3.0×

6 Level & World Design

6.1 Ground and Ceiling Layout

Ground Surface

  • Height: 100 pixels
  • Color: Dark brown (25, 20, 15)
  • Top highlight: (35, 30, 25)
  • Continuous solid platform

Ceiling Surface

  • Height: 100 pixels (mirrors ground)
  • Same color palette as ground
  • Inverted collision detection
  • Accessible via gravity flip

6.2 Procedural Generation

World Generation System

The WorldGenerator class creates infinite terrain ahead of the player while cleaning up passed segments to maintain performance.

Generation Rules

  • Terrain generates when camera approaches edge
  • Segments behind player are removed from memory
  • Enemy spawn points are distributed procedurally
  • Collectible placement follows weighted randomization

6.3 Environmental Hazards

While the primary challenges come from enemies, the environment contributes to atmosphere and spatial awareness:

  • Limited visibility in fog areas
  • Visual distractions from parallax elements
  • Narrow safe zones between enemy patterns

6.4 Atmosphere & Mood

Dark Night Setting

The game takes place in an endless nightmare landscape featuring:

  • Deep night sky gradient (near-black to dark purple)
  • Silhouetted dead trees and mountains
  • Drifting fog particles
  • Pale moon in the background
  • Sparse, eerie star field

8 Audio Design

8.1 Audio Philosophy

The audio design follows a AAA-style dynamic mixing system inspired by games like Dead Space, Limbo, and Inside. Sound reinforces the horror atmosphere while providing clear gameplay feedback.

8.2 Background Music & Ambience

Layered Ambient System

The AmbientMixer class manages multiple audio layers that crossfade based on gameplay intensity:

Layer Description Base Volume
Horror Ambience Unsettling drones, distant sounds 40%
Pond Ambience Natural, eerie outdoor sounds 35%

Dynamic Mixing

  • Volume levels adjust based on gameplay intensity
  • Smooth transitions (fade speed: 0.02 per frame)
  • Loops seamlessly without noticeable repetition

8.3 Sound Effects

Action Sound Description Purpose
Jump Whoosh / light impact Confirm action, spatial awareness
Shoot Sharp projectile fire Confirm action, feedback
Hit Enemy Impact / squelch Damage confirmation
Take Damage Pain / thud Alert player to danger
Gravity Flip Woosh / inversion tone Orientation feedback
Collect Coin Chime / pickup sound Reward feedback
Invisibility Activate Ethereal whoosh State change indication
Enemy Death Defeat sound / pop Elimination confirmation

8.4 Audio Settings

Music Volume (Default) 50%
SFX Volume (Default) 70%
Audio Channels 16 (reserved for mixing)

Procedural Sound Generation

If audio files are not present, the game generates procedural sounds programmatically to ensure audio feedback is always available.

10.4 Performance Considerations

  • Object Pooling: Reuse sprite objects to reduce allocation
  • Culling: Only update/render objects near camera
  • Procedural Cleanup: Remove off-screen world segments
  • Cached Calculations: Difficulty values cached until score changes
  • Sprite Groups: Pygame's optimized group rendering
